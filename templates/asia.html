Notable Locations

What are some notable locations in Asia? Well, given that there are so many countries, here is a brief rundown.

Mount Fuji: It is an active stratovolcano but it has not erupted since 1708. It is one of Japan's 3 Holy Mountains Dead Sea: The waters touch the borders of both Jordan and Israel. It is so thick, nothing can sink into it, and everyone who walks into it will naturally floa Phuket: It has some of the best beaches in the country. Beautiful coral reefs line up the coastline, offering amazing opportunities for snorkeling and diving but also sea kayaking and island hopping.

Beijing: It is one of the most populous cities in the world. It also has the eight-sided Tianning Temple, as well as almost 150 museums and galleries. Hoi An: It was a major commercial stop for trading ships as far back as the 15th century. Hoi An still maintains much of its original architecture Hong Kong: It is the third most densely populated place in the world. For the best views, visitors can climb to the top of Victoria Peak Singapore: This brand-new country is now a financial powerhouse and one of the most fun countries to visit in Southeast Asia. Kathmandu: It is home to holy monkeys and one of the oldest temples in Nepal. It is also a popular gateway to the Himalayas and to nearby destinations like the Ancient City of Patan. Jaipur: Known as the "pink city" because of the unique color of the stone used to build many of the structures around, Jaipur is also home to a community of artisans.
